About

He is widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen in the history of cricket.[4] He is the all-time highest run-scorer in both ODI and Test cricket with more than 18,000 runs and 15,000 runs, respectively.[5] He also holds the record for receiving the most man-of-the-match awards/ in international cricket.





Personal life

On 24 May 1995, Tendulkar married Anjali Mehta, a paediatrician of Gujarati origin, whom he had first met in 1990.[397][398][399][400] Anjali decided to leave her medical career following their marriage.[401] They have two children: Sara and Arjun.[402][403] Tendulkar lives in a bungalow in the Mumbai suburb of Bandra.[404] Tendulkar is a Hindu.[405] He is a devotee of the deity Ganesha[406] and of the guru Sathya Sai Baba, whom he visited for the first time in 1997.[407][408][409] The death of Sai Baba on Tendulkar's 38th birthday caused him to cancel his celebrations.[410][411]
